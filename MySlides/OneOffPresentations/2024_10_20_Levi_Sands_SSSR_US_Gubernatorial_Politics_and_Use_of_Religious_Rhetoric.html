## US Gubernatorial Politics and Use of Religious Rhetoric Levi Sands, MA, University of Iowa (levi-sands@uiowa.edu)
##
## Data - Project Vote Smart's (PVS) database of political speeches - Included all gubernatorial candidates for all elections from 2004 through March of 2023 - Data is from 10 states selected to represent a diversity of states in size, political control and regions of the country - AL, AZ, CA, ID, IL, ND, NY, RI, TX, WA - I used the Press Release, Speech, and Statement categories for a total of 34,192 "speeches" - A total of 77 politicians - For the purposes of analysis I split the data into sentences using pysbd (Sadvilkar and Neumann 2020) - Resulted in 855,440 total sentences
## Religious/Christian Dictionary Terms - I used religious terms then using regular expressions filtered to get just those sentences that contained one of the target religious terms - The terms I included were derived from Chapp (2012) and inclusions of my own - All included terms have a minimum of 50 occurrences (lowest number was 74) - I selected a random sample of each term to determine if at least each term had >85% religious usage of the term
## Religious/Christian Dictionary Terms - In plain English the terms used are below: - bibl, christ, christian, christianity, christians, christmas, christmases, church, churches, faith, god, holy, jesus, lord, pray, religio, sacred, worship - I used various methods to account for different forms of the terms - Example: to match with all forms of term, I used a word stem ("bibl") - Example: explicity used different forms of a term ("christ", "christian", "christianity") - In regex format: `bibl|faith|pray|religio|sacred|worship|(\b|\W|\s)(church|churches)(\b|\W|\s)|(\b|\W|\s)christ(ian|ianity|ians|mas|mases|\b|\W|\s)(\b|\W|\s)|(\b|\W|\s)god(\b|\W|\s)|(\b|\W|\s)holy(\b|\W|\s)|(\b|\W|\s)jesus(\b|\W|\s)|(\b|\W|\s)lord(\b|\W|\s)`
## Methods - Topic Modeling - Topic Modeling As a Method of Analysis - Several steps to get topic model outputs, each of those steps have tradeoffs: embedding, dimensionality reduction, clustering, tokenization, weighting scheme, and sometimes more (Grootendorst 2022) - Newer pre-trained embedding models use context to keep more semantic information (Devlin et al. 2019) - "'[T]opics' (clusters of words associated with a single theme) emerge inductively based on patterns of co-occurrence of words in a corpus." (Supran and Oreskes 2021 p 713)

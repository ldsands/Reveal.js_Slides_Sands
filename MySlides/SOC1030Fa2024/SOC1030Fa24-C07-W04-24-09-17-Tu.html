## Social Stratification - **Social stratification**: **hierarchical** arrangement of people within a society. - Stratification is a characteristic of society, and not a matter of individual difference. - Simplistic ways of measuring stratifications using Income and Wealth - **Wealth** is the value of assets minus the value of debts. - **Income** refers to money earned or received.
## What Happened? - Over the last few decades, the rich were getting richer, but the rates are not equal - The greatest contributors to the increasing inequality is the income earned by the very richest - Much of the increase in inequality comes down not to income (like a salary) but wealth which is taxed differently to income - "The Reagan Revolution": Reagan passed some tax reduction bills (trickle down economics), to promote an economic policy that has a long-lasting impact - These economic policies have largely continued over the subsequent decades

## International Context: Exploitation for Profits - How the Chocolate Industry Still Profits from Child Labor | ENDEVR Documentary ([on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eRc9hjejY0o)) - Coco in Ivory Coast where about 1/3 of the workers are children - After ~5-6 years workers are paid with a small parcel of land where they can then grow and sell the coco to get some money for themselves - Much bought by Cargill, they sell it to Hershey, Nestle and pretty much every other big chocolate maker - Cargill says that they buy coco from "ethical" growers (cooperatives) - "ethical" tracking is implemented at least in some places in Ghana (but not Ivory Coast)
## Why International Inequality - **Modernization Theory** - "rich nations became wealthy because early on they were able to develop the "correct" beliefs, values, and practices" (University of Minnesota, Libraries, and Publishing 2015) - Weber and the **Protestant work ethic**: Good fortune comes from hard work | financial success is a sign of personal virtue - Promotes individualism over collectivism, innovation, investment and exploitation - **Dependency Theory** - focuses on the "exploitation of these nations by wealthy nations" (University of Minnesota, Libraries, and Publishing 2015) - Core VS Peripheral Countries - **Core**: resources go in more advanced goods come out - **Peripheral**: natural resources and labor support the core countries - Colonialism, slavery, natural resources, funneling wealth back to colonizer - How to hide an Empire (Immerwahr 2019)

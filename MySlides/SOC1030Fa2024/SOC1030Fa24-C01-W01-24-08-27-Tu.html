A Little about Me

## How Do We Learn About Society? - Where do most of us learn about society? - Have we learned some thing about society that might be incorrect? - How do we KNOW anything about society?
## How I Approach Teaching This Course - I will present a lot of data - I use stories (anecdotes) to better communicate the data - I will be drawing on my experience and my areas of expertise - Religion - Argentina - Supreme Court - Theory is the explanation of the data
## Devil's Advocate and Freedom to Explore Ideas - In the class we will explore ideas that are controversial - I want all of you to give each other the freedom to explore ideas - Allow your classmates to say things that you disagree with - Group and class discussions are designed to be a place where you can learn through discussion - Backfire effect: If you attack you'll often only reinforce the idea you meant to change - I will frequently play "Devil's Advocate" with ideas you share so you can think through details and outcomes
(Amirloo et al. 2022)
## Misinformation and Vaccines - First mRNA vaccines tested in mice in the 90s, first human mRNA vaccine (rabies) in 2013 (Beyrer 2021) - IAR at USU did work on mRNA vaccine for Zika in 2017 (Richner et al. 2017) - Same year the first personalized neo-epitope mRNA vaccines (cancer) used in humans (Amirloo et al. 2022) - First mRNA vaccine with fatty envelopes (better for delivery) used for Ebola vaccine but Ebola didn't spread enough to need commercial development (Beyrer 2021) - West Nile Virus Vaccine
## Small Group Activity - In groups of 4 exchange contact information - Also share a little about yourself with your group - Where are you from? - Why go to Iowa? - What year are you? - What is your major? - What extracurriculars are you involved in (or hope to be involved in). - This is for if and when one of you are gone, you can get notes from class - Discuss the following questions as a group (write down notes on your group's responses, these notes are what will be turned in for participation activities) - What makes something a social problem? - What social problems you think most need to be addressed? - What social problems do you most want to discuss in class?

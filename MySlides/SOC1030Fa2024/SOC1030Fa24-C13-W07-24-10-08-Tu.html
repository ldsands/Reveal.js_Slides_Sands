## Social cohesion - Functionalist view: Emile Durkheim: Worried about society breaking down since not everyone was on farms anymore thus had different values, experiences, beliefs etc. - **Social cohesion**: the extent to which society holds together without dissolving into conflict. - Can think of this as how much trust there is in a group or having common goals, values etc. - **Collective conscience**: the common faith or set of social norms by which a society and its members abide - Examples: Sports teams - [What are some rituals that relate to sports?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pi_HiInBipc) - Religions and political parities are good examples of both - What are some groups that you think would have high social cohesion or have a collective conscience?

## Types of Solidarity - **Mechanical solidarity**: social cohesion is based on sameness (same values, beliefs, behaviors) -- agrarian society - Likely to get along with those like you - Example: you'll get along with those with the same or similar identities as you - **Organic solidarity**: social cohesion is based on difference and interdependence of the parts -- industrialized society - Likely to get along with those who you depend on or who depend on you - Example: you'll get along with your doctor, your kids' teachers etc.
## Solidarity and Justice - Associated with Mechanical solidarity: **Punitive justice**: makes the offender suffer and thus defining the boundaries of acceptable behavior, and unite the collectivity through punishment such as hanging, stoning, public shaming, etc. - Example: UN parking tickets from countries with intensive kinship narrow circle of trust, for them intent doesn't matter - The WEIRDest People in the World (Henrich 2020) - Associated with Organic solidarity: **Rehabilitative justice**: Rehabilitative responses are tailored to individual perpetrator, because of the varying circumstances and conditions. - Example: Think Boeing and all the issues with them, very few have been indicted for what they did with the 737 MAX
  • Where there is mechanical solidarity everyone has much in common with everyone else
    • Assumption of common knowledge, punishments are more outcome based
  • Where there is organic solidarity many rely on other individuals for the expertise
    • Assumption of different knowledge, punishments are tailored due to different circumstances
## Labeling theory - A symbolic interactionist approach to social deviance - **Labeling theory**: the belief that individuals subconsciously notice how others see or label them, and their reactions to those labels over time form the basis of their self-identity - Labeling theory argues that it is through the social process of labeling that we create deviance by assigning **shared meanings** to acts - **Shared meanings**: the common understandings that are socially constructed that are attached to some action, symbol, concept etc. - "Culture [or social boundaries] is actually 'enforced' through the **social sanctions** applied to those who violate **social norms**" (Andersen, Taylor, and Logio 2017)

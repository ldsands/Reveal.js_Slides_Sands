## Sociology and Network Science - Sociological Origins of Network Science - Early theory by Georg Simmel and Emile Durkheim - Some work done with operationalization in the 30s and 50s - In the 1970s developed into a science by Mark Granovetter and several others - Today some (relatively) famous Sociologists (Duncan J. Watts and Nicholas A. Christakis) have gone further with computation and network analysis - Freda Lynn - Sociology of Networks SOC:3880 - Only requires SOC:1010 or SOC:1030 - Also teaches Capstone and Methods course for undergrads - Yongren Shi - Data Science for Social Good - Also teaches this course - Both Dr. Lynn and Dr. Shi actively use network methods in their research - Dr. Shi and I have done some network science with Reddit (r/incels) using network methods
## Social Networks (Network Science) - **Nodes**: the distinct element or actor - The "dots" in network graphs - **Ties or Edges**: the connections between nodes - The lines connecting nodes - **Hubs**: Nodes with a high degree - Users with many ties (friends) also called bridging ties - Nicholas A. Christakis and sickness on campus - **Small-World**: "Easy" to get from any node to every other node in the network - The average degree of separation between 2 random nodes was 4.7 - Source: The Anatomy of the Facebook Social Graph - **Clustering**: to what degree the network is sorted into clusters - Facebook high clustering due to recommendation algorithm - How are clustering and social closure related? - **Homophily**: to what extent the ties are between nodes that are similar to each other - Example: "Mormonverse" on Reddit: automatic banned on r/lds if user has ever posted on r/exmormon
## Example Uses of Network Science - Those with a higher degree where more likely to get sick first (Christakis and Fowler 2010) - [Video of the spread of the Flu](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0012948#s5) - Academic publishing patterns (e.g. Biology publications have a much higher number of authors) [(Newman 2004)](https://www.pnas.org/doi/full/10.1073/pnas.0307545100) - The religiosity of the ties to the respondent impacts the depressive episodes of the respondent [(Upenieks 2020)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jssr.12668) - If the respondent is religious the more religious their ties the fewer depressive episodes they'll have - If the respondent isn't religious the more religious their ties the more depressive episodes they'll have - Quality of the ties in a network is tied to well being [(Offer 2020)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0022146520952767) - "Having a nondifficult partner is associated with lower loneliness compared to having no partner, but having no partner and having a difficult partner are related to similar levels of loneliness. Likewise, having difficult adult children and having no adult children are associated with reporting greater psychological distress than having nondifficult adult children."

## Effects of Poverty on Health - Most of the poor will live in areas that have low home and rent values (Desmond 2017) - Frequently these areas have lower values due to the presence of dirty industry - Example: cancer causing air pollution [ProPublica](https://projects.propublica.org/toxmap/) - These companies will have high levels of pollution nearby causing health problems for the local residents - Example: central (Rich) vs South Phoenix (Industry) (Bolin, Grineski, and Collins 2005)

## Effects of Water on Health - ~90% of available American fresh water is groundwater ([American Geosciences Institute 2017](https://www.americangeosciences.org/geoscience-currents/groundwater-use-united-states)) - Most aquifers are losing volume (due to human extraction) - Many of the chemicals we create eventually can and do leech into groundwater - Water treatment can remove many of these but that takes money and some are more expensive to remove than others

## Comparative Health Systems - Bismarck Model - In Germany, Japan, Belgium, Switzerland - Private health insurance companies - Financed through payroll tax and unemployed through federal insurance employers and employees pay through payroll deduction - Everybody is covered (this is the big difference between us and Bismarck Model) - Also insurers are not for profit (the other big difference between us and Bismarck Model)
## Comparative Health Systems - The Beveridge Model - Great Britain, Italy, Spain, Most of Scandinavia - Medical treatment is a public service - financed by the government through taxes = single payer - No medical bills - Public and private doctors (mostly public)
## Comparative Health Systems - National Health Insurance - Canada, Australia, Taiwan, South Korea - Health care providers are private, but he government-run health insurer is the payer = single payer - Every citizen pays into this insurance program
## Comparative Health Systems - Out-of-Pocket Model - Medical care paid for by the patient, out of pocket, with no insurance or government plan to help - Utah State University build on land that someone paid to a doctor for a surgery he had
## Comparative Health Systems - The US system - Working people under 65 (but not all) = Bismarck, but for profit - Native Americans, military, veterans = Beveridge Model - Over 65 = National Health Insurance - Uninsured = Out-of-pocket - Health Cooperative not for profit insurance and provider - This is a massive oversimplification

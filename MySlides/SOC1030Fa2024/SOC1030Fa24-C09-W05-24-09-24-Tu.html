## Social Reproduction of Inequality - **Social Reproduction of Inequality**: The basic idea is that social positions (e.g., social class, occupations, education, etc.) tend to pass on from one generation to another - **Social Institution**: institutions are social structures that have rules, norms and boundaries that influence the behavior of individuals. - Examples: Families, religion, government, schools, healthcare etc. - **Social Capital**: connections to people who have resources you can access - Examples: law or health expertise (doctors, lawyers), job prospects - **Cultural Capital**: class based knowledge and preferences used to signal your class to others - Examples: Opera (RBG), SNL Colbert etc., cars, etc. - **Social Closure**: group boundaries and identities that prevent others from using group resources - Examples: Country clubs, LDS humanitarian aid, Federalist Society etc.

## Co-Determination - "[I]nvolves the right of workers to participate in management of the companies they work for." ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Codetermination_in_Germany)) - McGaughey, Ewan, The Codetermination Bargains: The History of German Corporate and Labour Law (March 25, 2015) - Stakeholders differences and long-term vs short-term goals - managers, boards, shareholders, and workers - Shareholders want short-term investment returns - Stock buy backs - "when a public company uses cash to buy shares of its own stock on the open market." ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/advisor/investing/stock-buyback/)) Illegal from 1934-1982 ([Vox](https://www.vox.com/2018/8/2/17639762/stock-buybacks-tax-cuts-trump-republicans)) - Dividends - "payment in cash or stock that public companies distribute to their shareholders." ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/advisor/investing/what-is-dividend/)) - This money cannot then be re-invested into the company and employees - e.g. R&D, bonuses, wage increases etc. ([Curbing Stock Buybacks - RI](https://rooseveltinstitute.org/publications/curbing-stock-buybacks-crucial-step-raising-worker-pay-reduce-inequality/)) - Employees want long-term stability - "Enshittification (alternately, crapification and platform decay) is a pattern in which online products and services decline in quality. Initially, vendors create high-quality offerings to attract users, then they degrade those offerings to better serve business customers, and finally degrade their services to users and business customers to maximize profits for shareholders." ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enshittification))

Background

Corporate Social Responsibility

  • CSR frameworks (guidelines or standards) meant to incentivize companies to "do good"
  • They have been growing very fast in the last couple of decades
  • Two main CSR frameworks discussed here:
    • The UN Global Compact (GC)
    • The Global Reporting Initiative (GRI)

The UN Global Compact (GC)

  • The GC is meant to encourage self-reporting business activities in a variety of areas (see image on next slide)
  • There are "minimal requirements" associated with membership (see pg. 72)
  • Due to reporting being voluntary many can join but then not submit a report (these reports are called "Communications on Progress")
    • Companies that report to the GC are classified as meeting having "baseline commitment"
    • Those who join the GC but do not report are classified as having "ceremonial commitment"

Example of two columns

  • The GRI is much more restrictive and thus in order to be a member not only is reporting mandatory but it is also much more extensive that what is required by the GC
  • Those who are members of the GRI are classified as having "substantive commitment"

### Update on Fake Meat - Qualitative Coding - oppositional structures from Weber, Heinze, and DeSoucey (2008) - authenticity vs. manipulation - authentic: sincere, honest, transparent, connected - manipulation: instrumental, deceitful, obscured, decoupled - sustainability vs. exploitation - sustainability: Permanence, Nurturing, Conservation, Renewable resource - exploitation: destruction, depleting, extraction, commodity resource - natural vs. artificial - natural: pure, clean, healthy, complete - artificial: spoilt, dirty, unhealthy, fragmented
### Overall Summary of Codes Used by NCBA - authenticity vs manipulation: the NCBA mostly uses the authenticity vs manipulation codes by framing themselves as producing authentic meat while challengers are manipulative by using meat as a label on their "fake meat" product - natural vs. artificial: the NCBA frames their product as natural and nutritious whereas they state that "fake meat" is artificial and that it is either not nutritious or that challengers are overstating the nutritional value of "fake meat" - sustainability vs. exploitation: this is largely missing from the press releases that contain "fake meat" - I think that maybe the leveling the playing field could be coded in the exploitation code since it seems like the NCBA is framing the challengers as taking advantage of the meat brand/name/label to market their "fake meat"
### Other Possible Codes (NCBA) - fairness/justice - they often talk of leveling the playing field or fairness of the incumbents (cattle ranchers) vs challengers espcially in the labeling context - example: "encourage usda to look beyond modifying standards of identity in order to provide adequate protection for beef producers and consumers it is critical that the federal government step up to the plate and enforce fair and accurate labeling for fake meat said kevin kester president of ncba as long as we have a level playing field our product will continue to be a leading protein choice for families"
### Big National News Orgs - for more news type articles some of the NCBA's arguments are summarized - there is a lot of usage of environmental topics as a motivation for trying and eating regularly "fake meat" products - there is a lot of describing other issues with challenger's products such as price or as these products don't go far enough to address environmental/social justice issues
### Other Possible Codes (Big National News Orgs) - there are several uses of people making changes like eating "fake meat" for environmental purposes while ignoring other issues such as highly processed (i.e. bad for health) foods or not really cutting meat consumption, big companies getting tax breaks thus helping them in their bad (for environment or people) practices - example: "we have been trained from birth to enjoy them we will continue to eat cheeseburgers the easiest way for us to eat fewer cheeseburgers is to produce fewer cheeseburgers or at least to price them at their true cost one that includes their contributions to climate change public health environmental degradation and so on this would make them prohibitively expensive high tech fake meat isnt the answer because even though vegan ultra processed food doesnt kill animals it kills people" - the price of challenger's products is another topic I'm not sure what to do with - example: "i m also annoyed by the cost why pay more for fake meat than real meat especially since the production process is faster easier and involves no butchering and i have felt if you want to eat less meat why not just eat more of other real things" - heath may fit into sustainability but I think it may also stand as it's own code, e.g. "seeing growing consumer demand for less fat cholesterol and calories which often translates into a desire to eat less meat"

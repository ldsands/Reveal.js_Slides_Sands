### Apostates in Religious Literature - Two extremes: - Only those who leave a subversive organization (Bromley 1998) - "for people who report growing up with a religious identity yet now embrace none, apostasy is not too strong a concept. They have rejected their former identity in favor of no religious identification." (Sherkat 2014) - Apostate and other similar terms for religious exiters are framed as being deviant (Cragun and Hammer 2011) - Brooks (2018) showed that some are reclaiming the term - More research into this reclamation can provide insight into identity work (both individual and collective) of religious exiters

32.6.3.2

Apostasy

Issues of apostasy often have an impact beyond the boundaries of a ward or stake. They need to be addressed promptly to protect others.

The bishop counsels with the stake president if he feels that a member's action may constitute apostasy. The bishop or stake president may place informal membership restrictions on the member (see 32.8.3). The stake president promptly counsels with the Area Presidency. However, only the stake president decides whether a membership council or other action is necessary.

As used here, apostasy refers to a member engaging in any of the following:

  • Repeatedly acting in clear and deliberate public opposition to the Church, its doctrine, its policies, or its leaders

  • Persisting in teaching as Church doctrine what is not Church doctrine after being corrected by the bishop or stake president

  • Showing a pattern of intentionally working to weaken the faith and activity of Church members

  • Continuing to follow the teachings of apostate sects after being corrected by the bishop or stake president

  • Formally joining another church and promoting its teachings (Total inactivity in the Church or attending another church does not by itself constitute apostasy. However, if a member formally joins another church and advocates its teachings, withdrawing his or her membership may be necessary.)

The Savior taught the Nephites that they should continue to minister to a person who has sinned. "But if he repent not he shall not be numbered among my people, that he may not destroy my people" (3rd Nephi 18:31).

### Use of Apostate in r/exmormon - 58,706 posts containing some form of apostate/apostasy or heathen (June 2021) - As an individual identity - As a collective identity - As a label applied by others - As a label for actions/thoughts/behaviors - Used to confirm or refute stereotypes - Used to describe the perspective of TBMs
### As an Individual Identity - Apostate or heathen is used as an individual identity marker or label. This is frequently used as a celebratory or as a distinguishing identity from some other identity. > "Happy cake day fellow heathen. Enjoy your relationship untainted by the wedge of mormonism. I am truly happy for you." - Here is another example of using heathen as a identity to distinguish from another identity. Also, in this example the author is pointing out his previous commitment. > "I think I agree. I think it's funny that on my mission, I was the really good, super excited missionary. I followed all the rules to a T and hated if we weren't giving 110%. I had a companion that would sleep in through scripture study and talked about all the blowjobs he got before his mission. He's super active and an Elder's Quorum president while I'm a heathen."
### As a Collective Identity - Both apostate and heathen is used a collective identity label. It is here that pride in the label is most evident. > "Absolutely. That is part of the reason why some Atheists proudly wear a scarlet "A". When I started down the road to apostacy, the scarlet "A" sounded pretty good to me. By the time I arrived at an atheist, I wanted a pair of "A"'s."
### As a Collective Identity - Authors refer to common traits or behaviors of fellow apostates. Here the author points to a positive act/trait that apostates can use in interactions with TBMs. > "I'm of the opinion we shouldn't give them fuel for their bigotry, the more they realize that apostates don't descend into hedonism and remain good people, the more they'll question whether such accusations are true" - Unlike the above comment this submission asks what if apostates mobilize. > "Speaking in strictly hypothetical terms... What if? (Please, use your imagination!) What if we stopped being nice and stopped playing by their rules of obedient, reverent and non-confrontational apostasy? What could we do to fuel a level of protest within Mormondom equivalent to the civil rights or women's suffrage movements? Come on people, let's get creative!"
### As a Collective Identity - Authors also refer to how others (typically TBMs) treat apostates. > "Probably the mods are from the COB, and they have to keep the place clean from anything nice from apostates" > Oh we aren't allowed to be furious. No no no. Then we are just bitter angry apostates who left because we're offended or lazy or wanted to sin. We must force ourselves to smile and show TBMs just how happy we are to be out of the church. |LINEBREAK| For those of us whose spouses are still in? We must not be angry about anything to do with the church ever. Ever. If we show even the slightest glimmer then we are attacking that which they hold most dear.
### As a Label Applied by Others - In this submission the author assumes the ward members are applying the apostate label to the author. > So I wore jeans to church today. Why? Simple... I forgot to pickup my dry cleaning yesterday. I'm certain my neighbors probably think I'm going apostate.
### As a Label for Objects/Actions/Thoughts/Behaviors > I will drink apostate coffee, wear apostate sexy underwear, show my apostate shoulders, apostate knees and especially apostate inner thighs and underarms. |LINEBREAK| YEAH APOSTATE. |LINEBREAK| And the funny thing is that I never ever thought I was one. > "I had to look for that. ### Confirming/Refuting Apostate Stereotypes - Many posts are attempting to refute negative stereotypes surrounding apostates and apostate behaviors. > "Think being called an apostate is undesirable? Think again. Apostate means "escaped slave" in Greek" - Other posts confirm stereotypes. > "Would you rather the church be true or untrue? If you were to decide whether you would want the church to be real or not real what would you prefer? |LINEBREAK| I think I would prefer that it wasn't. I like the idea of being able to decide things for yourself, and not always having to appeal to this God. That's kind of similar to the Mormon idea of "apostates just want to sin," but if God were actually real I would still follow his rules. I would just prefer that instead of having some parental authority tell me I can't drink coffee I choose whether I want to or not myself. [...]"
### Taking on the Voice of TBMs - Authors write posts taking on the voice of a TBM. > I don't understand why this is a big deal. - Many years ago . . . > EXMO: Joseph Smith practiced polygamy. > TBM: That's a filthy apostate lie! > Many years later . . . > EXMO: Joseph Smith practiced polygamy. > TBM: That's a filthy apostate lie! > TSCC: Joseph Smith practiced polygamy. > EXMO: See? > TBM: I don't understand why you are making a big deal about this. I heard about it years ago! How can you say that the church was hiding this?
### Conclusions and Future Research Conclusions? - Exmormons are using the apostate/heathen labels to describe their identity - Exmormons are also redefining the the label to refute negative stereotypes or sometimes endorse stereotypes (often reframing them into something positive) Future Research - More work to be done on reclaiming the apostate label - Durable or liminal (Newfield 2020)? - Similarities between other exiters from other religious groups? - Investigate the "conversation" occurring between exmormons and church leaders and lay members. - Exmormon influence on wider views of Mormonism - Topic modeling and Natural Language Processing as a promising method for text analysis of Mormon texts.

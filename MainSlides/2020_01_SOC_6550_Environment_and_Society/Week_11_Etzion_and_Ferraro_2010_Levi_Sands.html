## Week 11 Summary Etzion and Ferraro 2010 12 April, 2020 Levi Sands Etzion, Dror, and Fabrizio Ferraro. 2010. “The Role of Analogy in the Institutionalization of Sustainability Reporting.” Organization Science 21(5):1092–1107.
## Institutional Entrepreneurship and Changing Institutional Culture - Institutional entrepreneurship is the process of trying to enact change in an institution. - This process is difficult because of two conflicting goals 1. changing current practices 1. not drawing ire those who have a vested interest in resisting any change
## Analogies in Institutions - An analogy is a figure of speech that is used to show the similarities between two objects or ideas - Analogies As Enablers Of Change - Analogies are used to help the process of institutionalization - This happens through linking a new practice with an already established practice - by using analogies the entrepreneur can effectively appear to be using legitimate "parts" used in other areas of the same or similar institution and create change - this allows the entrepreneur to implement change into an institution while framing it as something that is not as new or drastic as it could be seen otherwise - Analogies As Cognitive "Cages" - analogies have also been described as conservative as they limit potential exploration by framing something as being similar to some other established idea or practice - imagination and creativity has been shown to decline when an analogy is used in the introduction of a new idea (Dahl and Moreau 2002)
## GRI and Corporate Sustainability - The Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) is a nonprofit organization that is dedicated to institutionalizing sustainability practices and reporting worldwide. - Founded in 1997 with the goal of incorporating representation from all stakeholders in the development of their detailed reporting schema - Soon after its founding the organization moved from just environmental reporting to social and economic reporting as well
## Research Question - How does the use of analogy change as institutionalization occurs?
## Methods - Data Sources - Many different documents associated with the creation and modification of the GRI's sustainability guidelines over the years - Analysis method used - hand coding based on the principles of discourse analysis
## Results - Early on analogies were used frequently to establish legitimacy - they used the analogy of financial reporting often - they drew upon both similarities between financial reporting and contrasts or modifications to help drive change in the thinking of the corporations - Also early on they described the motivation in terms of beneficial financial consequences - By the time that the Third iteration of the guidelines were released there were many changes in the number and how analogies were used - Before the release of the third iteration of the guidelines t he GRI had become, "de facto standard for sustainability reporting" - This allowed the GRI to decrease dramatically reduce their use of the financial reporting analogies used in earlier iterations with great frequency - The use of linking to economic outcomes have all but been removed from this third iteration of the guidelines

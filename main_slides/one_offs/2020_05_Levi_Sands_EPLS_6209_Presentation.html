## Similar Surveys? a Tale of Three Surveys 5 May, 2020 Levi Sands EPLS 6209 Spring 2020 Presentation
## This is not a research presentation - As I created my first draft of the questionnaire I couldn't help but think about how different my survey might be from other surveys that are on this same(ish) topic - There are three surveys done with Ex-Mormons/former Mormons in the past few years - Since I will likely not continue with this exact research topic for many years I figured I should try to do something fun for this presentation - I will present questions from each survey that were meant to obtain the same(ish) information - I will ask each of you to try and match which set of questions belongs to which survey(or) at the end - I will also be taking a vote for which question you like the best for each of the questions I will be showing - Ideally I would like you to pick the question you think is the best according to the principles we learned in class - Poll Link - http://etc.ch/5CXv
## Contextual Background - The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (Mormon/LDS Church) - The largest branch of the Latter-day Saint movement started by Joseph Smith Jr. in upstate New York 1830 - Claims to have over 16 million members (Burningham 2011) - This number is very inflated as only about a third are "active" - Accelerating number of members becoming "disillusioned" - High-cost or demand religion (Scheitle and Adamczyk 2010) - "[H]igh levels of required participation, behavioral and social restrictions" - High cost of leaving the religion
## Surveys of interest - All surveys - Are targeted at Ex-Mormons - Were written in the last 4 years - Were written by academics - Were written by individuals who have identified as Mormons for multiple decades each - Are really long (~30 minutes or more) - All released to public (some not yet)
## Disclaimer - some of the questions have (very) slight wording changes to make it a bit more challenging - I usually just changed the tense to present - I did this just to keep the guessing interesting

## Week 7 Summary of Vasi et al. 2015 5 March, 2020 Levi Sands Vasi, Ion Bogdan, Edward T. Walker, John S. Johnson, and Hui Fen Tan. 2015. “‘No Fracking Way!’ Documentary Film, Discursive Opportunity, and Local Opposition against Hydraulic Fracturing in the United States, 2010 to 2013.” American Sociological Review 80(5):934–59.
## Fracking - Fracking is the process by which water is injected into shale rock to breakup the shale rock to release (primarily) oil and natural gas - Recently fracking has become much more prevalent due to improved technology and interest - the environmental impacts of fracking have been debated
## Gasland - A small documentary maker (Josh Fox) went to Colorado, Wyoming, Utah, and Texas and others to investigate the impact of fracking on the local populations - He was able to show some dramatic footage of being able to light some farmer's tap water - Gasland dramatically increased public interest and awareness in fracking ![Gasland](../../pics/SOC_6550_Environment_and_Society/Vasi_2015/gasland.jpg)
## Research Questions - Public discourse is not limited to mas media, thus previous research into these area did not account for media such as documentaries such as Gasland - Gasland afforded the authors an opportunity to analyze how social movements are affected by media that is not focused on mass media - Social media is another form of media that is distinct from other mass media in part due to the "echo-chamber" nature of the discourse - How are social movements formed or react to these forms of media
## Hypotheses - Hypothesis 1: Gasland’s release and award nominations increased (1) online public attention toward fracking; (2) social media chatter and mass media coverage; (3) discussion of water problems in social and mass media; and (4) discussion of bans and moratoria in social and mass media. - Hypothesis 2: Local screenings of Gasland will have a short-term effect on local mobilizations against fracking. - Hypothesis 3: Local screenings of Gasland will have a direct effect on the adoption of fracking bans. - Hypothesis 4: Local screenings of Gasland will have an indirect effect on the adoption of fracking bans, by contributing to local mobilizations against fracking.
## Hypotheses simplified - Hypothesis 1 upon Gasland’s release AND award nominations... - ↑ searches related to fracking - ↑ fracking social media posts - ↑ calls for bans on fracking (on social media) - Hypotheses 2 - 4 where Gasland is screened... - ↑ local opposition to fracking - ↑ fracking bans - ↑ fracking bans (through indirect means)
## Methods - Two analyses - How did Gasland impact the public discourse around the country? - How did Gasland impact local mobilizations against fracking? - They used Cox proportional hazards (a type of survival model) - Dependent Variables - social media discourse (used Google and YouTube searches, and Twitter posts that mention fracking related terms) - mass media discourse (newspaper mentiones of fracking) - local mobilizations (data taken from a website created to organize anti-fracking groups MarcellusProtest.org) - local fracking bans (municipal laws posted on Food and Water Watch)
## Methods cont. - Independent variables - Gasland screenings (locations and dates) they used distance to the screening - Controls - Proximity to previous adopters (100m; inverse sqr. dist.) - Twitter chatter, total number of messages (ln) - Population (ln) - Votes for Obama - Unemployment - Income (ln) - Marcellus location - Oil and gas industry - New York - Ohio - Pennsylvania - NGOs (all) - Environmental NGOs - Proximity to wells (five miles) - Proximity to water contamination (five miles)

The Upstream View

There I am standing by the shore of a swiftly flowing river and I hear the cry of a drowning man. So I jump into the river, put my arms around him, pull him to shore and apply artificial respiration. just when he begins to breathe, there is another cry for help. So i jump into the river, reach him, pull him to shore, apply artificial respiration, and then just as he begins to breathe, another cry for help. So back in the river again, reaching, pulling, applying, breathing and then another yell. Again and again, without end, goes the sequence. You know, I am so busy jumping in, pulling them to shore, applying artificial respiration, that I have no time to see who the hell is upstream pushing them all in.

Source: McKinlay 1979: 9

  • These companies will have high levels of pollution nearby causing health problems for the local residents

Obesity

  • Problem
    1. Obesity causes lots of money time and suffering throughout the US and obesity rates are about 1/3. Obesity causes diabetes which is a costly and painful disease
  • Action
    1. Shame those who are obese so that there will be a strong desire on the part of everyone to either lose weight or maintain a healthy weight
  • Why we should
    1. This will prevent many people from becoming obese or decrease the rates at which they gain weight, thereby preventing disease and the ill effects that come with it
  • Why we shouldn't
    1. There will be an increase in the rates of suicide, eating disorders, and poor self image for many americans that have difficulty with their weight

Comparative Health Systems

Bismarck Model
  • In Germany, Japan, Belgium, Switzerland
  • Private health insurance companies
  • Financed through payroll tax and unemployed through federal insurance employers and employees pay through payroll deduction
  • Everybody is covered (this is the big difference between us and Bismarck Model)
  • Also insurers are not for profit (the other big difference between us and Bismarck Model)

Comparative Health Systems

The Beveridge Model
  • Great Britain, Italy, Spain, Most of Scandinavia
  • Medical treatment is a public service - financed by the government through taxes = single payer
  • No medical bills
  • Public and private doctors (mostly public)

Comparative Health Systems

National Health Insurance
  • Canada, Australia, Taiwan, South Korea
  • Health care providers are private, but he government-run health insurer is the payer = single payer
  • Every citizen pays into this insurance program

Comparative Health Systems

Out-of-Pocket Model
  • Medical care paid for by the patient, out of pocket, with no insurance or government plan to help
  • Utah State University build on land that someone paid to a doctor for a surgery he had

Comparative Health Systems

The US system
  • Working people under 65 (but not all) = Bismarck, but for profit
  • Native Americans, military, veterans = Beveridge Model
  • Over 65 = National Health Insurance
  • Uninsured = Out-of-pocket
  • Health Cooperative not for profit insurance and provider
  • This is a massive oversimplification

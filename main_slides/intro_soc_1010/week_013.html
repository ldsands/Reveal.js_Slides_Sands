Social Networks

  1. A social structure made up of a set of social actors (such as individuals or organizations), sets of dyadic ties, and other social interactions between actors
  2. Individuals are interdependent and mutually construct and are constructed by their connections with others
  3. Structure of these connections shapes the flow of information, beliefs, and scripts for action

Social Capital

  1. A form of economic and cultural capital in which social networks are central
  2. Market agents produce goods and services not mainly for themselves, but for a common good
  3. Transactions are marked by reciprocity, trust, and cooperation
  4. Ability of group to maintain and enforce norms (informal social control)
  5. Diffusion of resources

Networks as Social Cohesion

  • Highlights the “good” social features of an individual’s environment (home, work, school…)
    1. Trust, reciprocity, information, social support/solidarity, social norms/sanctioning, emotional energy
    2. Associated with lower risk of distress (Phongsavan et al. 2006; Song and Lin 2009)
    3. lower mortality and higher selfrated health (Kim et al. 2008)
    4. fewer depressive symptoms (Almedom and Glandon 2008)
    5. lower rates of smoking, drinking, risky sexual behaviors (Lindstrom 2008)

What about negative effects?
